## What is the snake game?
> The snake game, also known as "Snake," is a classic arcade video game that originated in the late 1970s and gained popularity in the early 1980s. It has been implemented on various platforms and has many different versions.

In the game, you control a snake that moves around a grid or screen. The snake continuously grows longer as it consumes objects (often represented as "food" or "apples") placed randomly on the grid. The goal is to see how long you can make the snake grow without colliding with the boundaries of the play area or with itself.

The game typically has simple controls, allowing you to steer the snake in four cardinal directions (up, down, left, and right). As the snake consumes food, it gets longer and more challenging to navigate without colliding into itself.

Snake games have been implemented in various forms over the years, including on early arcade machines, feature phones, smartphones, and web browsers. It remains a `popular and nostalgic game that many people have played at some point in their lives.`

## Why did I do it? 
The Snake game is an iconic piece of gaming history that has entertained generations. Its simple yet addictive nature makes it an excellent candidate for a programming project. With this project, I set out not only to build a functional game but also to understand the underlying logic and mechanics that make it work.

I implemented the game with swipe controls so that mobile users can play the game without much hassle. Overall, this project was fun and I learned quite a bit.
